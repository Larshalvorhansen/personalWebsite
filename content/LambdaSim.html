While I can't reveal all the details just yet, get ready for a deep dive starting mid August 2024. In the meantime, here's a peek at what's driving this project.


Throughout human history, we have made remarkable progress in solving analytical problems. For example, in 1977, we successfully calculated the precise trajectories for Voyager 1 and 2, ensuring their successful journeys through space. Similarly, we have increasingly accurate weather prediction models, thanks to advanced computational techniques. However, while these problems are intricate andrequire detailed calculations, they aren't truly complex.


Complexity arises when there are nonlinear relationships, feedback loops, and unstable dynamics. Examples range from predicting traffic patterns in a bustling city to understanding the impact of climate change on global ecosystems.


Fortunately, we can make significant progress in predicting complex systems by breaking down each component into agents that follow a set of rules. This approach allows us to iteratively simulate the behavior of the entire system. By trying different values for the variables that we can change in real-world scenarios within the simulation, we gain valuable insights about how the system as a whole is affected. As a consequence, this insight enables us to make better, more informed decisions in the real world.


Examples of societal issues that could be simulated in this way include, but is not limited to:

  • Economic Stimulus Package: How a large economic stimulus, such as a package of tax cuts and public investments, affects economic growth, unemployment, and inflation over time.

  • Climate Quotas: The implementation of a national system for trading climate quotas and its impact on greenhouse gas emissions, industry, and the economy.

  • Pandemic Preparedness: The effects of various strategies for managing a pandemic crisis, including testing, tracing, vaccination, and lockdown measures, on both public health and the economy.

  • Tax Reform: The introduction of a new tax reform that changes tax rates for different income groups and businesses, and its impact on income distribution, consumption, and investments.

  • Public Transportation Expansion: The development of a new, comprehensive public transportation system in urban areas and its impact on traffic patterns, air pollution, and urban development.

  • Social Housing Policy: The effects of a large-scale public investment in affordable housing, and how this influences the housing market, urbanization, and social mobility.

  • Energy Policy: The transition to renewable energy through subsidies for solar and wind power, and its impact on energy prices, energy supply, and the environment.

  • Education Reform: The implementation of a reform that introduces free higher education, and how this affects student enrollment, education quality, and the labor market.

These examples illustrate how complex societal challenges can be modeled and analyzed through LambdaSim to support informed decision-making.
